Language - Grammar

Propositions may contain single line comments (// ... until end of line) and multi line comments (/* ... */). White space is not significant.

Grammar

Proposition ::= Atom | Const
| "(" UnOp Proposition ")"
| "(" Proposition BinOp Proposition ")"
Atom ::= ([letter]|"_"|"\"|"{"|"}")([letter]|[digit]|"_"|"\"|"{"|"}")*
Const ::= ("T"|"1"|"True"|"F"|"0"|"False")
UnOp ::= ("!"|"not")
BinOp ::= see Section below

In propositions with many brackets it is often difficult to see the matching ones. Therefore LWB allows to corresponding pairs of brackets: "(" ")" and "[" "]". Furthermore we economize on brackets by agreeing on precedence of operators: The unary operator binds more strongly than the binary operators. Binary operators have precedence according to the following section.

Binary Operators

Binary operators in LWB in descending order of precedence

Description Syntax Associativity
logical and "&", "and" left
logical not and "!&", "nand" left
logical or "|", "or" left
logical not or "!|", "nor" left
logical from "<-", "if" right
logical not from "nif" right
logical implies "->", "impl" right
logical not implies "nimpl" right
logical equivalent "<->", "iff" left
logical exclusive or "^";, "xor" left
